A little math game

Two people, one phone,
one rope.

Number Tug is an arithmetic tug-of-war you play on a single device. Answer math problems fast, and the rope slides your way. First one to yank it over the line wins.

Why I built this

I'm a phage biologist by day, but I have a soft spot for the kind of mental math that gets rusty the second you stop practicing it. I wanted a quick way to sharpen it up — something you could pull out while waiting for coffee and be mid-round in about three seconds. So I built a tug-of-war where the rope is the arithmetic and your thumbs are the muscle.

The best part turned out to be the two-player mode: two people, one phone held sideways between you, each guarding your half of the screen. It gets loud. There's a rope on the screen and it does not care about your feelings — it just slides toward whoever answers faster.

A fun, quick way to sharpen mental math. Two people, one phone, one rope — and a very smug winner.

What it does

🧍

Solo mode

Clear ten questions against the rope on your own. Just you, the arithmetic, and a stubborn piece of rope.

👫

Two players

Share one screen. Each player guards their side — tap the answer buttons or split the keyboard between you.

🤖

Versus the computer

No second human handy? Player 1 answers while the CPU works the other end of the rope.

⚡

Speed & streaks

Fast correct answers pull harder, and back-to-back correct answers build a streak bonus. Hesitation costs you.

🔀

Modes & difficulty

Add, subtract, multiply, divide, or mixed — across Easy, Medium, and Hard timers and number ranges.

🔊

Sound & haptics

WebAudio blips for hits, misses, and wins, plus vibration on supported phones. Toggle sound and music separately.

⌨️

Keyboard controls

Player 1 answers with A S D F, Player 2 with J K L ; — four keys per side for the four answer choices.

🙂

Names & avatars

Pick your name and face before the match, then test sound and haptics with one button so nothing surprises you mid-game.

How it works

  1. Set up the match

    Pick solo, two players, or vs CPU, choose your operation and difficulty, and name your player.

  2. A problem appears with four choices

    An arithmetic problem shows up with four answer buttons. Tap the right one — or hit its key — before the timer bites.

  3. Right answers pull the rope

    Each correct answer tugs the rope toward you. Answer fast and string together a streak to pull even harder; wrong answers and timeouts nudge it back.

  4. Yank it over the line

    Keep the pressure on until the rope crosses to your side. Then, of course, demand a rematch.

Built as a tiny TypeScript exercise

Number Tug started as a small excuse to practice React and TypeScript on something with real-time state and a bit of feel to it — the rope physics, the WebAudio blips, the haptics. It's built with React + TypeScript and Vite, and it's small enough to read end to end. I lean on AI assistance to move faster on the plumbing so I can spend the time on the parts that make it fun to play. The source is on GitHub if you want to poke around or borrow the rope trick.
